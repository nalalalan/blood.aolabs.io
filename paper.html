Blood: source-bounded personal anxiety scoring from glucose and wearable signals

Deterministic anxiety score, source freshness, HRV estimate boundary, and current live example.

Open PDF

Abstract

Blood turns automatic CONTOUR NEXT ONE glucose readings and Samsung/Health Connect heart rate, HRV, sleep, and steps into one visible stabilization score followed by a learned instability pattern and five aligned graphs. The anxiety estimate is not a diagnosis. It starts from a fixed raw baseline, adds or subtracts current source factors, scales the result to one decimal on a 1-10 range, and selects a stabilizing action from the strongest current outlier.

Current formula

Raw starts at 2.2. Stable glucose, calm heart rate, solid sleep, and high recent steps subtract from the score. Low glucose, high glucose, high heart rate, low HRV, short sleep, and low movement add to it. The visible score is clamp(1, 10, raw * 2), rounded to one decimal place.

Current live example

The June 27, 2026 live read reconstructs as 4.4/10: 82 mg/dL glucose, 68 bpm heart rate, 12 ms estimated HRV from sleep heart-rate samples, 431 minutes asleep, and 13,028 recent steps.

Current block

Blood names the current time block, the source reason, and one positive eating, drinking, or movement action from the strongest current outlier.

Pattern window

The top surface groups recent glucose, HR, HRV, sleep, and step records by Eastern time block, scores source instability with the same bounded factors used by the current estimate, and shows the block with the strongest recent pattern. The prediction recalculates from the latest uploads instead of using a fixed schedule.

Boundary

Heart rate is current only when the health bridge uploads and Samsung/Health Connect exposes current samples, so Blood shows both the health-upload time and source sample time. True HRV is used when source RMSSD exists. Otherwise Blood estimates HRV from dense low-noise sleep/rest heart-rate windows and labels the result as estimated. This paper is a system record, not medical advice.
